about ashley

In 2012, I created an account on the social media platform Tumblr. Curious about web design, I experimented with the HTML of my blog and was fascinated by the changes I could make. Utilizing online resources such as W3Schools, I taught myself front-end programming and started creating Tumblr themes for others to use.

In high school, I started taking computer science classes, learning Java in AP Computer Science and JavaScript in Introduction to Web Design. I spent summer 2016 interning at Professor Jun Liu's statistics lab at Harvard, where I learned to program in R.

Currently, I'm in my junior year at the University of Chicago, double-majoring in Computer Science and Economics with specializations in Machine Learning and Data Science. Since this summer, I have interned at the Computer Science branch of UChicago STEM Education, where I have developed two web apps and qualitatively coded over 640 sets of data.

On campus, I am heavily involved in multiple student organizations. I am the Operations Manager for compileHer, an organization dedicated to engaging middle school girls in computer science through hands-on activities; additionally, I am the Vice President of Fraternity Development for the Illinois Kappa chapter of Pi Beta Phi.
